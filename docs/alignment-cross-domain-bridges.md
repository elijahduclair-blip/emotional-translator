# Alignment Cross-Domain Bridges

## Purpose

This catalog connects the Theory of Alignment color-climate anchors through human myths, history, science, and arts.

The goal is not to prove that one symbol always means one thing. The goal is to give each concept a defensible bridge: a shared structure, an emotional climate, and a boundary that keeps interpretation from becoming endless.

Use this pattern:

`anchor -> shared structure -> human field -> emotional climate -> boundary`

## Bridge Rules

- Myth gives recurring story logic: descent, return, threshold, trial, blessing, curse, mask, devotion, exile, and transformation.
- History gives collective pressure: eras, rituals, revolutions, migrations, recoveries, technologies, and social roles.
- Science gives process logic: optics, weather, biology, chemistry, geology, ecology, entropy, adaptation, and systems.
- Arts give visible and sensory form: color, music, theater, image, architecture, costume, rhythm, genre, and composition.
- A bridge is strong when the same structure appears in at least two fields and produces a clear emotional climate.
- A bridge is weak when it only sounds beautiful but cannot name the shared structure.

## Field Examples

### Myth

Myth connects concepts through story pressure. It asks: what recurring human passage is happening here?

- Obsidian routes through descent: the climate of meeting shadow without pretending it is the whole self.
- Ember routes through fire theft or the forge: the climate of risk, heat, and transformation.
- Rose routes through Cupid or binding thread: the climate of being aimed at, attached, chosen, or pierced.
- Midnight routes through dream and oracle: the climate of listening when certainty has gone dark.
- Green routes through sacred grove and healing herb: the climate of protection, repair, and endurance.
- Silver routes through veil and threshold: the climate of partial seeing, ambiguity, and revision.

### History

History connects concepts through lived collective patterns. It asks: where has this pressure appeared in human time?

- Obsidian can map to exile, aftermath, and testimony: identity after rupture.
- Ember can map to revolt, industry, and migration: motion under pressure.
- Rose can map to letters, alliances, and mourning rituals: attachment made social.
- Midnight can map to navigation, vigil, and diaspora: orientation inside darkness.
- Green can map to agriculture, medicine, and restoration: survival through care systems.
- Silver can map to archives, negotiation, and transitional eras: truth assembled from incomplete records.

### Science

Science connects concepts through process. It asks: what real mechanism resembles this emotional movement?

- Obsidian is volcanic glass: heat cooled into a reflective edge.
- Ember is stored energy: a small glow that can become release.
- Rose is bloom and thorn: attraction paired with protection.
- Midnight is atmosphere and deep water: depth changes perception.
- Green is homeostasis: life balancing itself across changing conditions.
- Silver is mist and phase change: form shifting between states.

### Arts

Arts connect concepts through perception. They ask: how does this climate become visible, audible, or embodied?

- Obsidian appears as contrast, shadow, silence, silhouette, and negative space.
- Ember appears as rhythm, brass, flame, cuts, acceleration, and charged color.
- Rose appears as duet, lyric, floral image, softness, blush, and ache.
- Midnight appears as nocturne, blue note, wash, echo, and distance.
- Green appears as landscape, botanical line, repetition, grounding, and repair.
- Silver appears as montage, revision marks, grayscale, veil, and unresolved harmony.

## Connection Tests

Use these tests before accepting a bridge:

- Structural test: does the bridge share a real pattern with the anchor?
- Emotional test: does the bridge create a recognizable climate?
- Cross-field test: can the same pattern appear in more than one field?
- Boundary test: does the bridge describe a relational state instead of a fixed identity?
- Falsification test: what evidence or mismatch would make this bridge weaker?

Example:

`Obsidian -> volcanic glass -> underworld descent -> chiaroscuro -> exposure and reinvention`

This bridge is strong because the same pattern repeats: pressure, darkness, reflection, edge, and changed identity after descent.

Counterexample:

`Obsidian -> any sad feeling`

This bridge is weak because it skips structure. It names a mood without explaining pressure, exposure, shadow, or reinvention.
